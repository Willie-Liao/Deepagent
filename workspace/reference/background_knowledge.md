## Criterion A Overview

**Criterion A: Knowing and Understanding** assesses students' ability to use knowledge and understanding to solve problems and communicate ideas effectively. In the context of Physical and Health Education, this criterion evaluates how students apply theoretical knowledge and technical terminology to demonstrate comprehension of movement concepts, strategies, and principles.

---

### IB MYP Year 3 Criterion A Official Descriptors

From the IB MYP Physical and Health Education Guide (Page 6-7), Year 3 Criterion A: Knowing and Understanding has a maximum of 8 marks. At the end of year 3, students should be able to:

**i.** describe physical and health education factual, procedural and conceptual knowledge  
**ii.** apply physical and health education knowledge to explain issues and solve problems set in familiar and unfamiliar situations  
**iii.** apply physical and health terminology effectively to communicate understanding.

### Focus Areas for Physical and Health Education

In the MYP Physical and Health Education subject group, Criterion A specifically evaluates:

**Strand i: Use of Knowledge**

**Proper Terminology**: Students apply correct technical vocabulary when describing movements, skills, and strategies. For rope jumping, this includes terms such as Basic Jump, Boxer Step, Mummy Kicks, Side Swing, and High Knees.

**Communication in Unfamiliar Situations**: Students must communicate their understanding in contexts where the audience lacks prior knowledge of the subject matter. This requires clear explanations that do not assume shared background knowledge.

**Strand ii: Explanation of Choices, Structure, and Goals**

**Rationale Development**: Students provide reasoned explanations for why particular movements, sequences, or strategies were selected.

**Structure Analysis**: Students explain how the components of their routine are organized and why this organization supports the intended goal.

**Goal Alignment**: Students demonstrate understanding of how their choices align with specific fitness or performance objectives (endurance, coordination, calorie burn).

## The Poster Task (Week 3 - Criterion A Launch)

### Task Overview

**Goal:** Students create a visually engaging poster to teach their rope jumping routine to peers in an "unfamiliar situation"—meaning the audience has no prior knowledge of the student's specific routine or the technical details of rope jumping.

**Context:** This assessment occurs during Week 3 of the 6-week Artistic Rope Jump unit, immediately following the Criterion B planning phase. Students have already designed their routines and now must demonstrate their understanding by communicating that design to others.

**Purpose:** The poster serves as both an assessment of student knowledge and a peer-teaching tool. Other students will attempt to learn and perform routines based solely on the information provided in the posters.

---

### Task Parameters

**Format:** Visual poster (physical or digital) containing specific required elements mapped to Criterion A strands.

**Duration:** Week 3 of the unit (one class period for creation, one for peer review).

**Individual or Group:** Individual task—each student creates a poster for their own routine.

**Context Requirement:** The poster must be designed for peers unfamiliar with the student's routine, requiring clear explanations without assuming prior knowledge.

### Foundation Skills (Basic Level)

These skills establish fundamental rope jumping mechanics and are appropriate for all skill levels.

| # | Skill Name | Description | Difficulty |
|---|------------|-------------|------------|
| 1 | Basic Jump | Standard two-foot jump with consistent rhythm and form | Basic |
| 2 | Boxer Step | Alternating foot jump resembling a boxer's shuffle pattern | Basic |
| 3 | Rock/Rocker | Side-to-side weight transfer while maintaining jump rhythm | Basic |
| 4 | Basic Skip | Single bounce per jump with steady, controlled rhythm | Basic |
| 5 | March Step | Low-impact alternating foot pattern with minimal height | Basic |

---

### Dynamic Footwork (Basic Level)

These skills introduce movement variation while maintaining manageable coordination demands.

| # | Skill Name | Description | Difficulty |
|---|------------|-------------|------------|
| 6 | Run in Place | Jogging motion performed while jumping rope | Basic |
| 7 | High Knees | Elevated knee lift with each jump, emphasizing height | Basic |
| 8 | Mummy Kicks | Forward kick motion while jumping, alternating legs | Basic |
| 9 | Side Swipes | Lateral foot movement while jumping, shifting weight side to side | Basic |
| 10 | Side Straddle | Jump with feet apart then together in straddle pattern | Basic |
| 11 | Forward Straddle | Jump with forward-back foot scissoring motion | Basic |
| 12 | Bell Jump | Jumping forward and backward while maintaining rhythm | Basic |

---

### Intermediate Skills

These skills require increased coordination, timing precision, and technical proficiency.

| # | Skill Name | Description | Difficulty |
|---|------------|-------------|------------|
| 13 | Side Swing | Swing rope to the side without jumping, then resume | Intermediate |
| 14 | Side Swing to Jump | Side swing transition directly into a basic jump | Intermediate |
| 15 | Skier Jump | Side-to-side jumping motion with simultaneous foot movement | Intermediate |
| 16 | X-Foot/Scissor | Cross and uncross legs while jumping in scissor motion | Intermediate |
| 17 | Full Twist | 180-degree body rotation while continuing to jump | Intermediate |
| 18 | Heel Tap | Alternate heel touches to the ground while jumping | Intermediate |
| 19 | Single Leg Hop | Hop on one foot for multiple consecutive rotations | Intermediate |
| 20 | Speed Step | Rapid alternating foot pattern emphasizing quickness | Intermediate |

---

### Skill Selection Guidelines

**For Endurance Routines (3 minutes):** Students should select skills from the Foundation Skills and Dynamic Footwork categories (Skills 1-12) that can be sustained for longer durations.

**For Calorie Burn Routines (2 minutes):** Students may select from all Basic skills and lower-difficulty Intermediate skills that allow for interval-style execution.

**For Coordination Routines (1 minute):** Students may select Intermediate skills (Skills 13-20) that challenge motor coordination and technical precision.

---

## Three Routine Options (Skill Levels)

Students select one routine option based on their skill level and interests. Each option has specific requirements that align with different Criterion A focus areas.

| Skill Level | Duration | Focus | Movement Requirements |
|-------------|----------|-------|---------------------|
| **Beginner/Endurance** | 3 minutes | Steady pace, continuous execution | 3 skills from repository (Basic level) + 1 researched skill |
| **Intermediate/Calorie Burn** | 2 minutes | HIIT style, interval intensity | 3 skills from repository (Basic to Intermediate) + 1 researched skill |
| **Advanced/Coordination** | 1 minute | Fast pace, high technical difficulty | 3 skills from repository (Intermediate level) + 1 researched skill |

---

### Beginner/Endurance Option

**Focus:** Sustained aerobic activity and stamina building

**Structure:**

- Perform 4 movements as a combination
- Complete the combination twice
- Total duration: 3 minutes
- Pace: Steady, sustainable rhythm throughout


### Intermediate/Calorie Burn Option

**Focus:** High-intensity interval training effect

**Structure:**

- Perform 4 movements in interval format (high intensity followed by brief recovery)
- Total duration: 2 minutes
- Pace: Alternating between high-intensity bursts and active recovery periods


---

### Advanced/Coordination Option

**Focus:** Technical precision, speed, and complex motor patterns

**Structure:**

- Perform 4 movements at fast pace with minimal rest
- Total duration: 1 minute
- Pace: Rapid execution emphasizing technical accuracy under time pressure

### Week 5 Criterion C Implementation
General indicator: number of breaks; number of mistakes; length of time required; alignment with the plan. 
beginner level: they try to meet 3 minutes goal with their routine. Three 5-second breaks are allowed during the 3 minutes of time. The number of skips in each skill should match with that in the plan. If the time it takes to finish has to be shorter than 3 minutes due to quickness of skipping, the leeway should not be longer than 20 seconds.

Intermediate level: they try to meet 2 minute goal with their routine. Two 5-second breaks are allowed during 2 minutes of time. The number of skips in each skill should match with that in the plan. If the time it takes to finish has to be shorter than 3 minutes due to quickness of skipping, the leeway should not be longer than 10 seconds.

Advanced level: they try to meet 1 minute goal with their routine. No breaks is allowed during 1 minute. less than 4 times of mistakes are allowed to happen in one minute session.
